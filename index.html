West African Classic

Nigerian Jollof Rice

The ultimate one-pot celebration rice — smoky, deeply flavoured, and absolutely irresistible. A staple at every Nigerian party table.

Nigerian Party Jollof Rice — the smoky, beloved classic.

Recipe Details

Prep Time

20 mins

Cook Time

50 mins

Total Time

70 mins

Servings

6 people

Difficulty

Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 5 medium ripe tomatoes, blended
  • 3 red bell peppers (tatashe), blended
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo), blended
  • 1 large onion, half blended, half sliced
  • ½ cup vegetable or sunflower oil
  • 2 cups chicken or beef stock
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 2 seasoning cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. 1

    Blend the tomato base: Combine the tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnets, and half the onion in a blender. Blend until smooth, then set aside.

  2. 2

    Fry the tomato paste: Heat the oil in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden. Pour in the blended tomato mixture and cook, stirring occasionally, for 25–30 minutes until the raw tomato smell disappears and the paste darkens and reduces.

  3. 3

    Season the base: Add the thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir well and cook for another 3 minutes to let the spices bloom in the oil.

  4. 4

    Add stock and rice: Pour in the chicken or beef stock and bring to a boil. Wash the parboiled rice, drain, and add it to the pot. Stir once to ensure the rice is fully coated in the tomato base.

  5. 5

    Steam to perfection: Cover the pot tightly with foil first, then the lid — this locks in steam. Reduce the heat to very low and cook for 25–30 minutes. Do not lift the lid during this time. The rice is done when it is fluffy and has absorbed all the liquid.

  6. 6

    Get the "party" smoky bottom: In the last 3–5 minutes, remove the foil and increase the heat briefly to high. This creates the coveted slightly smoky, caramelised bottom layer — the hallmark of true party Jollof Rice.

  7. 7

    Rest and serve: Turn off the heat and let the rice rest for 5 minutes before serving. Serve hot alongside fried plantain, coleslaw, grilled chicken, or beef.

Tips & Notes

A few pro tips to guarantee the best results every single time:

  • Always fry the tomato base long enough — undercooked tomato paste is the number one cause of a sour or flat-tasting Jollof.
  • Use parboiled rice for best results. Regular rice can turn mushy if you're not careful with liquid ratios.
  • The foil-and-lid method is the secret to even, non-burnt steam cooking. Don't skip it.
  • For a deeper smoky flavour (the "party Jollof" effect), cook over a wood fire or briefly expose the pot bottom to high direct heat at the end.
  • Leftovers taste even better the next day once the flavours have fully melded.
